First-Line Bladder Cancer Clinical Trials

Start Date Study Name or NCT Company Sponsor N (enrollment) Specific Patient Population Active Arm Control Arm Primary Endpoints Comments
Nov 2, 2015 DANUBE (NCT02516241) AstraZeneca 1032 Untreated unresectable Stage IV urothelial carcinoma; included both cisplatin-eligible and -ineligible patients (carboplatin allowed) Durvalumab monotherapy; Durvalumab + Tremelimumab Platinum-based chemotherapy (gemcitabine + cisplatin or carboplatin) OS (PD-L1–high subgroup); OS (all patients) Dual checkpoint immunotherapy vs chemo. Co-primary OS endpoints were not met – neither durvalumab alone nor with tremelimumab improved survival vs chemo.
Apr 2016 JAVELIN Bladder 100 (NCT02603432) Pfizer / Merck KGaA 700 Unresectable locally advanced or metastatic UC with no progression after 4–6 cycles of first-line platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine chemotherapy Avelumab maintenance + best supportive care (BSC) Best supportive care alone (observation) OS First trial of switch maintenance immunotherapy after chemotherapy. Demonstrated significantly prolonged OS with maintenance avelumab vs observation, establishing a new standard of care in 1L maintenance.
Jun 30, 2016 IMvigor130 (NCT02807636) Genentech/Roche 1213 Previously untreated locally advanced or metastatic UC; both cisplatin-eligible and -ineligible patients included (cisplatin or carboplatin per investigator's choice) Atezolizumab + platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine; Atezolizumab monotherapy Platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine + placebo PFS; OS First positive Phase 3 combo immunotherapy trial in 1L UC. Atezolizumab + chemo significantly improved PFS vs chemo, but did not yield a significant OS improvement at final analysis. Atezolizumab monotherapy arm showed no OS benefit over chemo, leading to use restricted to PD-L1–high/cis-ineligible patients.
Sept 15, 2016 KEYNOTE-361 (NCT02853305) Merck (MSD) 1010 Untreated locally advanced or metastatic urothelial carcinoma; included cisplatin-eligible and -ineligible patients (cisplatin or carboplatin allowed) Pembrolizumab + platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine; Pembrolizumab monotherapy Platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine (standard chemo) PFS; OS Three-arm trial (PD-1 monotherapy and combo vs chemo). It failed to show a significant improvement in PFS or OS for the pembrolizumab + chemo arm compared to chemo; consequently the pembrolizumab monotherapy arm was not formally tested for OS superiority. No new approvals stemmed from this negative study.
Mar 24, 2017 CheckMate 901 (NCT03036098) Bristol Myers Squibb / Ono 897 Previously untreated unresectable or metastatic urothelial carcinoma; trial had cohorts for cisplatin-eligible and cisplatin-ineligible patients (a) Nivolumab + cisplatin/gemcitabine (with maintenance nivolumab); (b) Nivolumab + Ipilimumab Platinum-based chemotherapy (gemcitabine + cisplatin for eligible, or + carboplatin for ineligible) OS; PFS Multi-arm study. In cisplatin-eligible patients, nivolumab + chemo significantly improved OS and PFS vs chemo. However, in PD-L1≥1% cisplatin-ineligible patients, nivolumab + ipilimumab did not improve OS compared to chemo. The chemo–immunotherapy combination arm led to regulatory approval, while the dual immunotherapy arm was negative.
Sept 27, 2018 NILE (NCT03682068) AstraZeneca 1246 Untreated unresectable locally advanced or metastatic urothelial carcinoma; both cisplatin-fit and -unfit patients (cisplatin or carboplatin per eligibility) Durvalumab + platinum/gemcitabine; Durvalumab + Tremelimumab + platinum/gemcitabine Platinum + gemcitabine chemotherapy OS (originally in overall and PD-L1 high populations) Trial of adding immunotherapy (PD-L1 ± CTLA-4) to standard chemo. After initial data, protocol was amended to focus on PD-L1–high patients. Ongoing trial (primary completion expected 2025); no final results yet reported as of 2025.
May 29, 2019 BeiGene BGB-A317-310 (NCT03967977) BeiGene 420 Locally advanced or metastatic urothelial carcinoma with no prior systemic therapy; cisplatin-eligible or -ineligible (must be fit for either cisplatin or carboplatin) Tislelizumab (PD-1 inhibitor) + platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine Placebo + platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine OS Global double-blind trial of PD-1 inhibitor from China in 1L UC. Designed to test addition of tislelizumab to platinum/gem chemotherapy vs chemo alone. Trial is ongoing (no results yet).
Mar 2020 EV-302 / KEYNOTE-A39 (NCT04223856) Seagen / Astellas + Merck 886 Previously untreated locally advanced or metastatic urothelial carcinoma; regardless of cisplatin eligibility (cisplatin or carboplatin allowed in control arm) Enfortumab vedotin (Nectin-4 ADC) + Pembrolizumab Platinum (cisplatin or carboplatin) + gemcitabine chemotherapy PFS; OS Chemo-free novel combination. This trial showed enfortumab vedotin + pembrolizumab significantly improved OS and PFS over standard chemotherapy in first-line advanced bladder cancer. Led to FDA approval (Dec 2023) of the combo as a new first-line option, regardless of cisplatin eligibility.
